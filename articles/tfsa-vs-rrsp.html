TFSA vs RRSP: Which should you prioritize?

By Carrie Wang, CEA, CLU®

5 min read · Published April 2026

Choosing between a Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA) and a Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP) is one of the most common questions Canadians face when planning their financial future.

The Tax Treatment Difference

The primary difference lies in when you pay taxes. With an RRSP, you get a tax deduction now, but pay tax when you withdraw the money. With a TFSA, you contribute after-tax dollars, but your withdrawals—including all growth—are completely tax-free.

When to Prioritize an RRSP

  • You are in a high tax bracket now.
  • You expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
  • You want to use the Home Buyers' Plan for a first home.

When to Prioritize a TFSA

  • You are early in your career or in a lower tax bracket.
  • You need flexibility to withdraw funds for short-term goals.
  • You have already maximized your RRSP contributions.
"The best strategy often isn't choosing one over the other, but understanding how they work together to minimize your lifetime tax bill."
