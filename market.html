Carrie's market context

What current conditions mean for your plan
Interest rates

Rate cuts are good news for borrowers — but act thoughtfully

With the Bank of Canada cutting rates from 5.00% to 2.75%, mortgage renewals and variable-rate borrowers are seeing relief. But lower rates also mean GIC and savings yields are falling — making a diversified investment strategy more important than ever.

Retirement & investing Read insights
Inflation

Inflation back near target — what it means for your retirement savings

With CPI at 2.3%, inflation has returned close to the Bank of Canada's 2% target. This is positive for fixed-income investments and retirement income planning — but long-term planners should still account for inflation eroding purchasing power over 20–30 years.

Retirement planning Read insights
Equities

Global markets remain resilient — staying invested matters most

Despite ongoing uncertainty, diversified equity portfolios have rewarded patient investors. Trying to time the market is rarely effective — a consistent, long-term strategy aligned with your risk tolerance and time horizon will outperform short-term reactions.
